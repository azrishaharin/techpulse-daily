Okay so, fourteen years. That's how long it's been since Guild Wars 2 launched, and the entire time ArenaNet has been quietly updating, expanding, and sustaining it while the MMO crowd wondered if a third entry would ever materialize. Now we have our answer: Guild Wars 3 was officially revealed at Summer Game Fest, and yeah, my hype meter just spiked.

From the reveal trailer, this isn't your typical MMO. ArenaNet is leaning hard into action-style combat, but the real star appears to be traversal and momentum. The trailer opens with the player character riding a majestic wolf-like mount across sweeping landscapes before hopping off to scale cliffs on foot, which gives me serious Breath of the Wild energy. The world looks expansive — like, genuinely massive — and there's a brief tease of a party of four meeting up atop a mountain peak, which suggests the cooperative structure that defined the series is sticking around. You can wishlist it right now for PS5 and Steam, and the beta is officially slated for Fall 2027.

What's wild is that Guild Wars 2 is still kicking. The Visions of Eternity expansion in 2025 proved ArenaNet can still deliver meaningful content to a 14-year-old live game while presumably building a sequel in the background. That's a tough needle to thread, and honestly it's a bit of a flex.

The big question now is whether Guild Wars 3 will keep the no-subscription model that made the series such a fan favorite, and how much of Guild Wars 2's sprawling lore carries over. Details are thin, but after waiting this long, I think we can stomach a few more months of speculation before that 2027 beta.
