Okay so, I know what you're thinking — 5% off a $10 gift card is basically a fancy way of saying "here, have fifty cents." And honestly? You're not wrong. But hear me out, because Amazon's Prime Day gaming deals actually have a sneaky little play here that might be worth your time.

Both PlayStation and Xbox $10 eGift Cards are sitting at 5% off, and the Nintendo eShop gift card is even better at 10% off the same $10 amount. The best part? You don't need to be a Prime member to grab these deals, which feels almost rebellious in an age where every retailer wants your monthly subscription just to let you look at a sale. Amazon's basically saying "we already have your money, what's another few bucks" — and for once, I respect it.

Now here's where it actually gets interesting. You can buy two of each card at a time, which is the move Amazon is clearly hoping you'll make. Grab one for yourself, one for a friend, and suddenly that 5% discount starts making a little more sense. It's essentially Amazon pre-solving your birthday shopping problem for the next few months. Got a gamer in your life who already owns everything? Gift card. Don't know what game to finally pull the trigger on during these sales? Gift card. The lack of decision fatigue alone is worth something.

Is this the kind of deal that goes viral? No. Will it change your financial future? Definitely not. But if you're already browsing Prime Day with a vague sense that you should be spending money on something gaming-related, this is a low-effort way to lock in a small saving before the whole thing wraps up. Sometimes the unsexy deals are the ones you actually use.
