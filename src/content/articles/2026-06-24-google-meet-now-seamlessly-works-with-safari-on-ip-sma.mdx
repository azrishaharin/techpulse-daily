Okay, how many times have you scrambled to hop on a Google Meet call only to realize the device in your hand is an iPhone without the app installed? Yeah, me too. That awkward little dance of downloading an app while everyone else is already talking is finally coming to an end.

For a while now, joining Google Meet on an iPhone meant you needed the Meet app or Gmail installed first. If you were in a pinch — say, you grabbed the nearest device when a meeting suddenly kicked off — you'd be stuck watching a download progress bar while the call got started without you. Google is fixing that by rolling out mobile Safari support, which means iPhone users can now join calls directly from a link in their browser. No app install, no fuss.

Even better, you don't need to be logged into a Google account to join. Tap the link, type in your name, and you're in. It's a small change on paper, but it's the kind of friction removal that actually matters when you're rushing from one thing to the next. The whole point of a video meeting tool is that it's there the second you need it — not two minutes later after a trip to the App Store.

Honestly, this is one of those updates that probably should have shipped a long time ago, but I'm not going to complain about it arriving now. The next time my calendar ambushes me, I'll be grateful to just tap a link and join. Fingers crossed the browser-based experience holds up once real-world bandwidth and shaky Wi-Fi get involved.
