Is "Sunny days sweeping the clouds away" about to get a feature-length treatment on Netflix? It sure looks that way. The streamer has reportedly won a bidding war against Universal Pictures for the rights to develop a Sesame Street movie, and honestly, I'm a little surprised it took this long.

According to The Hollywood Reporter (and as first reported by The InSneider), Netflix picked up the film rights from Sesame Workshop, the nonprofit behind the iconic children's show. Producing the project alongside Sesame Workshop is Rideback, a production company founded by Dan Lin — who, fun fact, joined Netflix as its head of film back in 2024. Rideback's résumé includes live-action versions of Lilo and Stitch and Aladdin, so they clearly know their way around translating beloved properties for modern audiences.

The big question now: what will the movie actually look like? Details are still under wraps, including which filmmaker might take the reins. We don't even know yet how classic characters like Elmo, Big Bird, and Cookie Monster will fit into a feature-length story. That's a lot of "TBD" for a property this beloved, and frankly, the lack of a director attached suggests this is still pretty early in development. So don't expect a release date announcement anytime soon.

Still, the move makes sense for Netflix. Family-friendly content is one of the few categories where streaming services can still feel like they have something to offer, and Sesame Street carries decades of built-in nostalgia with parents and genuine love from kids. If anyone can pull off a big-screen version without ruining the magic, I'd put my money on the team that's already been shepherding these characters for half a century.
