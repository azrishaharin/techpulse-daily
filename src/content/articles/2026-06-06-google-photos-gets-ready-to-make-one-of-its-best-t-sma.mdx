Okay so I had no idea Google Photos had a collage maker buried in there, and apparently I'm not alone. Most of us fire up Photos to scroll through memories or do a quick crop, but tucked away inside the app is a whole collage tool that honestly deserves more attention. And it looks like Google finally agrees, because they're prepping a pretty meaningful upgrade for it.

From what Android Authority has dug up, Google Photos is working on a fresh set of categories for its collage templates. Right now the options are pretty limited, so giving users more organized ways to build collages based on themes or occasions should make the feature way more approachable. On top of that, there are signs that border editing tools are in the works too, which would let you tweak the look of your creations beyond just slapping photos onto a grid. It's a small thing, but it's the kind of polish that turns a hidden feature into something people actually open on purpose.

Then there's the more interesting wrinkle. Code in the app hints at something called "Soba," which appears to be an AI-powered video feature. Details are still thin, and we don't really know what it'll do just yet, but the name alone has me curious. Google has been pushing AI into just about every corner of its apps, so seeing Photos get another smart tool isn't surprising, but I want to know what kind of videos it's generating and how much control we'll have over the output.

Honestly, I'm rooting for both of these. Collages getting better categories is a no-brainer win, and whatever Soba turns out to be could be the kind of thing that finally pulls people away from third-party editing apps. Keep an eye on this one.
