Okay so this one's a genuine time-sensitivity alert if you're a founder sitting on a Startup Battlefield 200 application. TechCrunch is officially closing the window on June 8 at 11:59 p.m. PT. That's three days. Not three weeks, not "sometime this summer" — three days.

I know the temptation to keep polishing that pitch deck for another week is real, but at some point the marginal improvement on slide 14 stops being worth the risk of missing the deadline entirely. The whole point of Startup Battlefield is getting in front of investors, press, and a room full of people who actually pay attention to early-stage companies, and you can't do any of that from your couch wondering what could've been.

If you're unfamiliar: Startup Battlefield 200 is the qualifying round for TechCrunch Disrupt 2026, which lands at Moscone West in San Francisco this October. The 200 companies that make the cut get to compete for a shot at the Disrupt Stage — the same stage where the likes of Dropbox, Mint, and Cloudflare all kicked off their origin stories. It's not a guarantee of anything, obviously, but the alumni list is the kind of thing that makes VCs actually open your cold email.

My honest take? The Disrupt application is one of the few in the startup world where the asymmetric upside actually justifies the awkward "please describe your traction in 280 characters" portion. Worst case you spent a weekend writing. Best case you end up on a stage that has launched real companies. And with the deadline falling on a Sunday night at the end of a holiday weekend, I wouldn't be surprised if more than a few founders talk themselves into thinking they have until Monday. They do not.

Get it in.
