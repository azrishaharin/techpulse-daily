Okay so, you've just sat through two hours and thirteen minutes of Masters of the Universe. Your bladder is screaming. The credits start rolling. Do you bolt for the exit or stick it out? Based on what I've been reading, you absolutely want to stay seated.

He-Man fans, this is the moment we've been waiting for since Dolph Lundgren suited up in 1987. The franchise is finally getting another shot at the big screen in live-action, and from the sounds of it, the people behind this Travis Knight-directed film aren't treating it like a one-and-done experiment. Amazon MGM Studios, Mattel, and Escape Artists clearly see this as a launching pad. Why else would they bother hiding bonus scenes after the credits? That's a move that says, "we've got more story to tell and we want you invested enough to wait around and see it."

And honestly, with a runtime that long, you should know going in that you're signing up for the full theatrical experience, credits and all. The post-credits scenes are apparently enough of a draw that film folks are making a point to tell audiences not to leave early. That's a pretty confident statement from a studio, and it suggests there's something worth seeing in those final moments.

My guess? We're probably getting a tease for whatever comes next, whether that's a direct sequel or a wider world being built out. These post-credits stings have become the standard playbook for franchise-building, and Masters of the Universe has decades of lore to pull from. Skeletor, Eternia, the whole cosmic mythology. There's plenty of gas in the tank.

So yeah, skip the pre-show bathroom break, minimize your drink runs, and plan to hang around. You'll thank yourself when those final moments hit.
