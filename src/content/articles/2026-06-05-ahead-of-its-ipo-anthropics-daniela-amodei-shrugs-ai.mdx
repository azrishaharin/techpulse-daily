Okay so, when a company's annualized revenue jumps from roughly $9 billion to $47 billion in about five months, you don't need a finance degree to know something significant is happening. That's the numbers Anthropic just put on the board heading into its IPO — and co-founder Daniela Amodei is essentially telling the skeptics to take a seat.

Let me put that growth in perspective. Crossing $47 billion in annualized revenue in May 2026 is the kind of trajectory that turns a startup story into a market-shaping event. Going from $9 billion at the close of 2025 to where they are now isn't just growth — it's a near-vertical line on the chart. Demand for their AI products and API usage is clearly outpacing even the optimistic forecasts people were throwing around a year ago.

But here's the thing Amodei seems to be acknowledging with that shrug: the easy part might be behind them. The "real test" framing in the original report isn't subtle. Going public means quarterly earnings calls, investor scrutiny, and answering hard questions about whether AI spending is actually translating into lasting economic value — or whether the industry is riding a wave of hype that eventually flattens out. Every AI company faces that question right now, but very few are doing it with a $47 billion revenue run rate to defend.

What I'll be watching is how the market digests this once the IPO dust settles. The first few earnings reports will tell us whether that growth curve keeps bending upward or whether the law of large numbers finally starts to bite. Either way, Amodei's confidence signals that Anthropic isn't planning to slow down — and that's a story worth following closely.
