Okay so, I have to admit — when I first heard that Final Fantasy Tactics: The Ivalice Chronicles was getting a New Game+ mode, my immediate reaction was a slow, confused blink. Ramza's story is one of the most tightly written tragedies in the entire Final Fantasy catalog. Do you really need to replay it with overleveled party members? Apparently Square Enix thinks so, and fans are already going back and forth on whether this addition actually enriches the experience or just lets you steamroll through a story that hits hardest on your first painful run.

The patch dropped on June 17 as Update 1.5.0, and the headline feature is exactly that: New Game+, which the original 1997 release (okay, 1998 in English, my bad) never had. You can now carry over your units and inventory from a finished save into a fresh playthrough, which means you can keep grinding those same soldiers all the way up to the Level 100 cap. For completionists, that's basically an open invitation to turn the late game into your own personal playground.

Beyond NG+, the update sneaks in a bunch of smaller quality-of-life stuff that frankly should have been there from day one. You can finally see a unit's status effects at a glance instead of squinting at tiny icons, and there's now an option to strip all gear off a character in one go — small thing, huge time saver when you're juggling the job system.

But here's the part that has people grumbling: the War of the Lions content is still missing. The PSP remake added extra jobs, characters, and cinematics, and remaster owners have been waiting. For now, it remains a "maybe someday" situation.
