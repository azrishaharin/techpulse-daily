So a plane had to turn back last week because someone's Bluetooth speaker had a custom name that triggered a security scare. Let me say that again — a Bluetooth speaker. Not a bomb, not a suspicious package, just a speaker with a funny name. The passenger had apparently named the device something that sounded alarming enough to prompt an evacuation and a full cargo search. I don't know what the name was, and honestly, I don't want to know. But this whole situation made me laugh and also think about how absurd our relationship with technology has become.

It got me thinking about all the other little tech annoyances we put up with daily. Take under-display fingerprint scanners, for instance. I saw a piece on Tech Advisor this week where the author basically said they're over it — and I get it. Eight years ago, in-display scanners felt like the future. No button, just tap and go. But the reality is that they can be sluggish, they struggle when your fingers are slightly damp or cold, and honestly, a good old-fashioned fingerprint button often just works better. Yet manufacturers keep pushing the under-display version like it's still a selling point. It's not. It's a compromise dressed up as innovation.
