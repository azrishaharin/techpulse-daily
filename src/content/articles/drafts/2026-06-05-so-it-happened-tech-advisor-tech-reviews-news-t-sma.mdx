I still remember the first time I unlocked a phone with my fingerprint sitting directly on the screen. It felt like magic—like something out of a sci-fi movie. Eight years later, Anyron Copeman's opinion piece on Tech Advisor about being done with under-display fingerprint scanners hit me hard, because honestly? He's right. The technology was supposed to simplify our lives, but more often than not, I'm tapping my fingerprint three or four times before giving up and typing in my PIN. The promise never quite matched the reality, and it's one of those small frustrations that somehow makes using a premium device feel clunky.

While we're on the subject of things that haven't lived up to the hype, Google's latest moves are worth a closer look. The Pixel 11 wallpapers have started leaking, and they suggest a shift in Google's colourway strategy. After years of playful, pastel options, it looks like Google might be heading toward something more sophisticated—or at least more muted. Whether this reflects a design evolution or just a desire to stand out from the sea of gradient smartphones remains to be seen, but I'm curious to see how Pixel fans react. Alongside this, Googlebooks is reportedly getting a refresh, and one of the models is apparently a tablet. If Google is serious about competing in the tablet space, that's a bigger deal than it might seem at first glance.
