Oi, phone lah, you all still using that old sluggish device or already upgraded to something with decent camera and battery life? Actually, the latest smartphones are not just for Instagram stories and scrolling TikTok anymore — they're turning into legit fitness partners, and honestly, it's pretty cool how far we've come.

Tom's Guide just dropped their Fitness Awards 2026, and guess what? Many of the top picks this year work seamlessly with your smartphone. We're talking about smartwatches that track your sleep like some kind of sleep detective, fitness trackers that count your steps and remind you to get off your butt, and even massage guns that connect to apps on your phone to customize recovery sessions. It's like having a personal trainer in your pocket, minus the expensive gym membership.

The newer smartphones now come with built-in health features that used to require separate gadgets. Heart rate monitoring, blood oxygen levels, stress tracking — all these used to be on dedicated devices, but now your daily driver phone can handle it. Pretty convenient right? Plus, the battery life on recent flagships means you can track your entire day without worrying about finding a socket.

But here's the thing — not all phones are equal when it comes to health features. Some have better sensors, some have tighter integration with third-party fitness apps, and some just give you basic data without the fancy analysis. Tom's Guide tested tons of gadgets and smartphones, and the ones that actually deliver are the ones with proper health ecosystems.

So if you're planning to upgrade soon, maybe consider how your phone fits into your wellness routine. Because let's be real, we already spend so much time on these devices, might as well let them help us stay healthy too. At least that's what I'm telling myself when I use my phone to track my morning jogs instead of actually running faster.
