Looks like Samsung’s next flagship is already turning heads before it even drops. Leaked specs for the Galaxy S26 Ultra are floating around, and the big news is a 200 MP camera sensor—yes, 200 megapixels—taking mobile photography to a whole new level. If the rumors are true, this phone will let you crop, zoom and still keep crazy detail, perfect for those “wow, that shot looks like a DSLR” moments.

But the camera isn’t the only thing getting a boost. Samsung is also said to be cooking up a new AI assistant that lives right inside the phone’s OS. Think of it as a built‑in helper that can handle everything from setting reminders to suggesting the best angle for a selfie, all without you lifting a finger. The assistant is rumored to learn your habits over time, so it’ll get smarter the more you use it.

The rest of the hardware sounds pretty solid too. Expect a 6.8‑inch Dynamic AMOLED display with a 120 Hz refresh rate, the latest Snapdragon 8 Gen 4 (or Samsung’s own Exynos equivalent depending on region), up to 16 GB of RAM, and a 5,000 mAh battery that could keep the phone running all day. Fast charging is said to hit around 100 W, so you can go from empty to full in under half an hour—handy when you’re always on the go.
