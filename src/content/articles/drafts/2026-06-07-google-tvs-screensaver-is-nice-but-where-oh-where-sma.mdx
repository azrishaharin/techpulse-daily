Okay, so I have a confession: I am weirdly obsessed with my TV's screensaver. I know, I know — that sounds ridiculous. But hear me out. Google TV's Ambient Mode isn't just some logo bouncing around a black screen. It's a full-on art gallery. We're talking images from NASA, Getty, and Google Arts & Culture, all quietly cycling while my TV sits idle. It looks gorgeous, and as a bonus, it keeps the display from burning in.

But here's the part that makes it genuinely special: it pulls photos straight from my Google Photos library. Travel memories, family moments, that random sunset I took in Portugal — all of it rotates across the screen like a personal slideshow I'd never bother to set up myself. It's the kind of feature you didn't know you needed until you had it.

And that's exactly the problem. Ambient Mode works so well that I keep instinctively trying to open Google Photos on my TV to browse a specific album, then I remember... I can't. There's no native Google Photos app on Google TV. Just the ambient slideshow, doing its thing without you. It's like being handed the world's best appetizer and then being told the restaurant doesn't actually have a kitchen.

The gap is strange when you think about it. Google owns both products, both tie into the same account, and the ambient mode already proves the integration is technically possible. The software is already pulling and displaying my photos. Surely adding a full app — one where I could browse albums, start a manual slideshow, or flip through vacation pics with friends over — can't be that much of a stretch.

For now, I'll keep enjoying my accidental art gallery. But Google, if you're listening: please build the rest of the restaurant.
