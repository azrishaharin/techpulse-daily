Okay so Android 17 barely had time to hit stable last week before Google started rolling out the next round of test builds. Two weeks after QPR1 Beta 4 dropped, Beta 5 is now live for Pixel testers to dig into.

This one is mostly a cleanup release. Google's changelog is loaded with bugfixes rather than flashy new features, which honestly makes sense at this stage — we're talking about the quarterly release that will eventually land as part of the September Feature Drop, so polishing things up before the final cut is exactly what testers want to be doing. If you're running a beta on your daily driver, this should feel more stable than previous builds rather than introduce a bunch of new quirks.

The other notable change? Pixel 6 and Pixel 6 Pro owners are back in the mix. Beta 4 oddly skipped support for those two devices, but they're included again in Beta 5. If you've been sitting on the sidelines waiting for that, go grab build CP31.260608.007 and start poking around.
