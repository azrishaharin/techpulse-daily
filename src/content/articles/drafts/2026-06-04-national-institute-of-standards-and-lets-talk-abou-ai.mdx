Ever wonder who’s behind those super‑precise atomic clocks that keep your phone’s time on point? That’s the National Institute of Standards and Technology—aka NIST—turning 125 years old this year and still the quiet workhorse of American tech.

NIST is the United States’ official metrology institute, meaning it sets the gold standard for measurements that everything else depends on. From the first atomic clock that made GPS possible to today’s encryption algorithms that keep your banking app safe, NIST’s fingerprints are all over the tech you use daily.
