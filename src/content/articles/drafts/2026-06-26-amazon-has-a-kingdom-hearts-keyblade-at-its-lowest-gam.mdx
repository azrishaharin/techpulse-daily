Okay, Kingdom Hearts fans — how long are we actually willing to wait for Kingdom Hearts 4? Because Square Enix finally showed off new gameplay footage earlier this month, and surprise: there's still no release date anywhere in sight. So while we sit here refreshing trailers like it's a part-time job, Amazon is dangling a very different kind of consolation prize.

Here's the thing — the Kingdom Hearts series has built up one of the most loyal fandoms in gaming, and for good reason. Square Enix cooked up something genuinely special when it mashed up classic Disney characters with a totally unique RPG formula. That kind of chemistry doesn't come around often. So you'd think Prime Day would be the perfect time for Square Enix to hook fans up with discounts on the actual games, right? Wrong. None of this year's Prime Day Square Enix deals include any of the KH titles.

But! There is a 3-foot stainless steel Keyblade replica that just dropped to its lowest price ever on Amazon. It's the blade Sora swings around in every game, scaled up to something you could absolutely bonk a stranger with at a convention. Nobody genuinely needs a sword, sure, but owning a piece of merch from a series that defined your childhood is a different kind of purchase — it's a vibe. With Kingdom Hearts 4 still floating in development limbo, sometimes a replica is the closest thing to a win you're going to get.

Honestly, this is the kind of deal that says a lot about where the series sits right now. The games keep drifting further away, but the merch pipeline never sleeps. If you're a fan, this Keyblade might just be the only Kingdom Hearts-shaped thing you get to unbox this year.
