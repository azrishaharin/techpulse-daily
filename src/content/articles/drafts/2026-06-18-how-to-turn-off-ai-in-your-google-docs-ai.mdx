Okay so, the "Help me write" pop-ups in Google Docs — we've all seen them. That little Gemini suggestion sliding in every time you start typing, cheerfully offering to finish your sentence like an overeager coworker. If you've been quietly annoyed by them, you're not alone. According to TechCrunch, there's finally a way to make those pesky "write with Gemini" prompts go away, and honestly, it's about time someone walked users through it.

Here's the deal: Google has been pushing its AI features pretty hard across Workspace, and Google Docs has been one of the main playgrounds for Gemini. That means anytime you open a blank document, there's a decent chance you'll get a Gemini suggestion box nudging you to let AI take a crack at your writing. For some people, that's helpful. For others — especially anyone trying to actually focus and write their own thing — it's an interruption. TechCrunch published a guide walking through the steps to disable those prompts, and the whole process apparently lives in your Google Docs settings. A few clicks, toggle off the AI assistance, and the pop-ups stop showing up. No need to uninstall anything or jump through weird hoops.

The interesting question is what this signals about Google's bigger AI push. The company clearly wants Gemini everywhere — in Docs, Gmail, Sheets, you name it — but it's also giving users an off switch. That feels like an acknowledgment that not everyone wants AI woven into every keystroke, and forcing it too aggressively could backfire. If users feel like they're being nagged, they'll find a way to mute it anyway, so it makes sense for Google to make that process simple rather than letting people get frustrated.

If you've been side-eyeing that "write with Gemini" banner every morning, now you've got a fix. Check out the full walkthrough on TechCrunch for the exact steps.
