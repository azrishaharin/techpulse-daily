Okay, so Google really, truly cannot stop reinventing the "point at something on your screen and search for it" trick. This week, the company quietly pushed a new "Select from screen" option into Gemini in Chrome, and at this point I'm half expecting a fresh variation to drop every quarter just to keep the streak alive.

The premise should feel familiar by now. Chrome users have had Google Lens for grabbing on-screen content and running a search, so this isn't a brand new idea. But the latest tool is built specifically for handing content over to Gemini. When you're chatting with the AI assistant in Chrome and want it to see what's actually on your page, you'll get a new option to scoop a piece of the screen and pass it along. The feature surfaced on Google's Gemini in Chrome support pages earlier this week, which suggests it's either rolling out now or about to.

Here's the part that gets me, though: the pile-up is getting silly. Google Lens is still around. Circle to Search is still around. Now Select from screen in Gemini is here too. It's the same core idea wearing a slightly different outfit each time. Sure, the flows aren't identical — Lens leans into visual discovery, Circle to Search is a gesture, and this one is about giving Gemini context — but the underlying impulse is the same.

Is that a problem? Not really, as long as each tool actually pulls its weight. But I do wonder when Google will just pick one and commit. For now, Chrome users get another option in the toolbox, and the count keeps climbing.
