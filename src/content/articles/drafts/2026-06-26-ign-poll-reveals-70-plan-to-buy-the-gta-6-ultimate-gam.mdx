Okay so let me get this straight: the base version of GTA 6 is $80, doesn't even include multiplayer, and somehow 70% of people in IGN's poll are saying they'd rather pay $100 for the Ultimate Edition. I had to read that twice. Earlier this week, IGN put up a simple question to their readers: which edition of GTA 6 are you actually planning to buy? The $80 Standard Edition or the $100 Ultimate Edition? After thousands of responses rolled in, the answer landed, and yeah, it's not what I expected. The vast majority are willing to shell out the extra twenty bucks.

For some quick context: Rockstar has confirmed that GTA 6 will set you back $79.99 at minimum, which is already a $10 jump over the $69.99 price point that's dominated this console generation. That's before you even get to play online with anyone. The Ultimate Edition, meanwhile, costs $99.99, and Rockstar is dangling some genuinely tempting exclusives to justify that jump, including access to exclusive shops and even an exclusive mission that Standard buyers won't get to touch. It's the kind of value gap that's almost designed to make you feel like you're missing out if you go cheap.

Look, I get it. GTA 6 is probably the most anticipated game of the decade, and Rockstar knows it. Whether that's a good thing for the rest of us is a different conversation, but the fact that 70% of gamers polled are perfectly fine with a $100 price tag tells you everything you need to know about how much pull this franchise has. The real question now is whether other publishers will follow Rockstar's lead, or if GTA 6 just gets to break the rules because it's GTA 6.
