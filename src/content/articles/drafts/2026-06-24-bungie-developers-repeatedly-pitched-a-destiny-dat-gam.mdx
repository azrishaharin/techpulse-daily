Okay so, imagine working at Bungie and spending your free week trying to pitch a Destiny dating sim to leadership — and getting shut down every single time. That's apparently the story here, and honestly? I kind of love it.

Former Bungie community member Liana Ruppert dropped this little nugget of gaming trivia after spotting a fan online wishing for a Destiny dating sim. Her response was golden: the team actually built a prototype, but leadership gave what she described as "a very hard no," apparently convinced that nobody wanted romance or silliness in the Destiny universe. She noted it had a real Dream Daddy vibe, which is about the most wholesome comparison you could make.

Then former Bungie senior narrative designer Robert Brookes jumped in to fill in the backstory. Apparently, the concept was born during one of those internal events where the studio lets devs prototype fun side projects for a week. Sounds amazing, right? The catch is that a small group of developers kept trying to scale it into a full-blown project, and that's where leadership kept slamming the brakes. Brookes' take basically amounts to: yeah, the dating sim was real, but it was a scrappy little jam-week thing, and the rest is lore at this point.

It's the kind of behind-the-scenes story that makes you wonder what else is sitting on the cutting room floor at major studios. Game jam projects get pitched all the time, and most of them die in a Slack channel. But this one had actual fans almost getting it, and Bungie still said no. Maybe the suits didn't want to dilute the serious space-magic tone, or maybe they just thought it would flop. Either way, somewhere out there is a Destiny dating sim that will never see the light of day — and that's both hilarious and kind of sad.
