Okay so, I just watched the trailer for Games Workshop's new Warhammer 40K animation and all I can think is: someone needs to turn this into a full game immediately. Like, yesterday. Because what we're seeing here is basically a proof of concept for a first-person Chaos Space Marine experience, and I am fully here for it.

For context, Space Marine 2 absolutely crushed it last year with its third-person action. Saber Interactive nailed the weight, the brutality, the whole "you are an unstoppable engine of war" vibe. But there's always been this nagging question in the back of my head: what would that feel like in first person? Seeing a Chainaxe tear through a loyalist Marine from behind your own eyes? Sign me up. And now, thanks to a new animation called The Butcher's Nails, we finally get a taste.

Debuted during Games Workshop's Big Summer Preview showcase, the gory short follows Makrath, a World Eaters Berzerker who throws in with a squad of Iron Warriors for an assault on loyalist Space Marines backed by Astra Militarum infantry. The trailer is almost entirely from Makrath's POV, and it's genuinely disorienting in the best way. You see the rip-and-tear from inside the helmet, the spray, the chaos, the close-up savagery. It's visceral in a way that third-person just can't replicate.

The Butcher's Nails is heading to Warhammer TV soon, and honestly, I think Games Workshop might be testing the waters here. If this thing gets the response I think it will, the pitch meeting for "first-person Space Marine game" suddenly gets a whole lot easier. Someone at Focus Interactive or Saber, please pick up the phone. My mouse hand is ready.
