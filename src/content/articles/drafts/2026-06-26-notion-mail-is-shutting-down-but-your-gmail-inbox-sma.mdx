So Notion is pulling the plug on Notion Mail. Yep, the database-meets-inbox experiment is officially winding down, with the AI-powered email client set to shut its doors on September 22, 2026. If you actually use it, you've got a little under a year to figure out your next move.

I remember when Notion Mail first dropped — it felt like a genuinely fresh take on email. Instead of treating your inbox like a chaotic to-do list, Notion wanted you to organize it the way you'd organize a Notion page. Custom inbox views, filter rules, AI-generated labels, snippets for quick replies, and granular notification preferences — the whole pitch was turning Gmail into something closer to a productivity dashboard. It was ambitious, and honestly, kind of cool in theory.

But ambitious doesn't always equal sustainable, and Notion's announcement feels like a quiet admission that email is a brutal space to break into. If you're still relying on Notion Mail, the company is asking you to export your settings before that September 22 deadline. That includes your inbox views, AI labels, snippets, and notification preferences — basically everything that made the app feel like yours. Worth doing sooner rather than later so you're not scrambling at the last minute.

The bigger story might be what this says about AI email tools more broadly. Every big platform is racing to bake AI into your inbox, and somewhere in the middle, standalone apps like Notion Mail are getting squeezed. Notion's database philosophy is still great for docs and projects — but your inbox, it turns out, might not have wanted a rebrand after all.
