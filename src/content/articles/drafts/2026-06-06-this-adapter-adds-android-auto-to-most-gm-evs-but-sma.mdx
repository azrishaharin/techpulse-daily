Okay, so let me get this straight — General Motors has spent years refusing to put Android Auto and CarPlay in its electric vehicles, and now a third-party company is stepping in with a nearly $200 adapter to fix what GM won't? Welcome to 2026, where the aftermarket still finds a way.

The device is called the EV Play LT, made by a company called EVPlay that already produces similar adapters for other cars. It plugs into most Chevrolet, GMC, and Cadillac EVs from the 2024, 2025, and 2026 model years, and it adds both wired and wireless support for Android Auto and Apple CarPlay — the two features GM has stubbornly refused to build into its EVs in the first place. The pitch is simple: if the carmaker won't give you what you want, maybe a $200 box will.

But here's the part that should make you pump the brakes. GM has taken what I'd call an unusually hard stance on this, and that raises a real question about whether the automaker could push a software update down the road that quietly breaks the EV Play LT. Third-party adapters live and die by what the manufacturer does to the infotainment system, and GM has shown zero interest in playing nice with anyone else's ecosystem.

So is it worth it? Honestly, if you've been skipping the Silverado EV or the Cadillac Lyriq specifically because you refuse to give up Waze in your dashboard, this might be the workaround you've been waiting for. Just know you're basically gambling $200 that GM doesn't flip a switch six months from now.
