Did Sony just set the tone for the rest of the year? The June 2 State of Play stream hit the ground running, delivering a surprise punch that many of us weren’t expecting so early in the summer. As the first major showcase of the season, it gave us a glimpse of what’s coming to PlayStation consoles, and let me tell you, the lineup feels both familiar and freshly daring.

The biggest headline from the event is a brand‑new God of War title. After the emotional gut‑punch of the last entry, this sequel promises to dive deeper into Norse mythology while keeping the brutal combat that made the series a household name. If you’re like me, you’ve been replaying the original for nostalgia, and now the prospect of a fresh adventure has you clearing your schedule before the game even has a release window. The teaser was short but packed with cinematic flair, showing Kratos in a snow‑covered battlefield, surrounded by towering runes. It’s clear that the developers are eager to expand the mythos without losing the intimate storytelling that hooked fans in the first place.
