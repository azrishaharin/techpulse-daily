Okay so here's a question I've been chewing on lately: how do you actually know if an AI agent is any good before you unleash it on the real world? Patronus AI thinks it has an answer, and the market clearly agrees — the startup just raised $50 million to build "digital worlds" specifically designed to stress-test AI agents.

Founded by former Meta AI researchers, Patronus is a company I've been quietly watching because it sits in a corner of the AI world that doesn't get nearly enough attention. While everyone's racing to build smarter agents, this team is focused on rigorously breaking them. The premise is pretty intuitive: create simulated environments where agents can be thrown into the kind of weird, unpredictable scenarios they'll eventually face in production, and see where they fall apart. It's the agent equivalent of a crash-test dummy lab.
