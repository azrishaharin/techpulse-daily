Okay so, I've been playing Devil May Cry games since I was a kid, and learning that the Netflix anime is wrapping up after Season 3 genuinely stings a little. But honestly? I kind of love that showrunner Adi Shankar is owning the decision instead of letting it look like a cancellation. He basically just told everyone: this is exactly how it was always supposed to go.

The news dropped via IGN, and Shankar came out swinging with one of the most confident showrunner statements I've read in a while. The show, he explained, was designed from day one as a three-season arc, and if you've been paying close attention to the episode titles, you already had the whole blueprint. Season 1 was Inferno, Season 2 was Purgatorio, and now Season 3 will be Paradiso. That's Dante's Divine Comedy with guns and a red coat. He even gave the whole run a name: "The Force Edge Saga." Since inception, Shankar says, it was built as a movie trilogy disguised as a television series. You have to respect that kind of long-term storytelling confidence in the era of shows that drag on for six seasons past their expiration date.

Here's the thing, too: the timing isn't a desperate move. Devil May Cry Season 2 just dropped last month and the reception has been stellar, which means they're not ending it because it flopped. They're ending it because the story is done. That's a luxury most Netflix shows never get. I can't tell you how many series I've watched limp into a final season with a half-written script because someone upstairs pulled the plug.

So yeah, it sucks that we're losing new episodes of Netflix's coolest action anime, but at least we get a real ending instead of a cliffhanger and a prayer. Bring on Paradiso.
