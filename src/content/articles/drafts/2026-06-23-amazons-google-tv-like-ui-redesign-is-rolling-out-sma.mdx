Okay so, did Amazon just quietly admit Google TV was doing it right? The redesigned Fire TV interface — one that looks suspiciously similar to Google's living room layout — is finally rolling out to current-generation Fire TV Sticks and Ember smart TVs as of today. That means if you've got a Fire TV 4K Select, a Fire TV Cube, or one of those Ember TVs Amazon launched last fall, you should be seeing the new look any moment now, right on the eve of Prime Day.

The overhaul was first revealed near the beginning of the year, and at the time it was pretty obvious Amazon had been peeking over at Google TV for inspiration. The old Fire TV interface was a wall of rectangular tiles that felt dated the second you opened it. The new one leans harder into a content-forward layout with bigger artwork and a more focused row structure, which honestly is overdue. Fire TV has been one of the best-selling streaming platforms in the world precisely because the hardware is cheap, not because the software was a joy to navigate.

Amazon says it plans to bring the new experience to other devices across several manufacturers worldwide, which matters because Fire TV OS powers a lot of third-party sets and sticks that aren't made by Amazon. The wide rollout is what'll really determine whether this redesign lands or fizzles — a slick UI on first-party hardware is nice, but most people are watching Fire TV on a budget Toshiba or Insignia.

My main curiosity is whether this is just a cosmetic refresh or if Amazon is actually rethinking how it surfaces content. The old interface shoved Prime Video to the front in ways that felt heavy-handed, and any move toward a more neutral discovery experience would be a real shift. We'll see soon enough.
