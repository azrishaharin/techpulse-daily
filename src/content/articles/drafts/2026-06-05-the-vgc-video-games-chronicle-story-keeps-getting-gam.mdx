Remember when everyone said the Switch 2 would flop? Nintendo just proved the doubters wrong again, and the numbers are honestly kind of shocking. The console hit one million units sold in the UK a full 30 weeks faster than the original Switch did, making it Nintendo's second-fastest selling home console in that region. That's massive, especially when you consider how crowded the gaming market is right now. But here's where things get interesting — over in Japan, Switch 2 hardware sales dropped a staggering 87% week-on-week after Nintendo raised the price. The surge before the increase was real, but once that sticker shock hit, Japanese consumers apparently stepped back. It's a classic case of demand meeting reality, and it makes me wonder if we're seeing the beginning of a cooling-off period or just a temporary dip before the holiday season picks up again.

On the software side, Switch 2 owners finally have something to celebrate. Elden Ring: Tarnished Edition has been stuck in development limbo for months, with the port originally planned for 2025 before delays pushed it back for "performance adjustments." Well, FromSoftware finally confirmed a release date, and it's happening soon. I played the original Elden Ring on Switch and honestly, it was rough around the edges, so I'm curious whether the team has managed to make this version feel polished. If they pull it off, it could be a real system-seller for Nintendo's newest hardware.

Speaking of Nintendo, there's apparently a Nintendo Direct coming next week, just after Summer Game Fest wraps up. The last one was nine months ago, so the drought has been real. I'm expecting Switch 2 game announcements, maybe some updates on that rumored revised model with the replaceable battery that Europe is getting due to new legislation. With the console selling so well in some markets and struggling in others, Nintendo needs to keep the momentum going with software.
