How long are you willing to wait for a game? Because ConcernedApe just gave us another "still working on it" update for Haunted Chocolatier, and honestly? I get it now.

Eric Barone — the one-man studio behind Stardew Valley — dropped a new blog post titled "Still here, still grinding…" where he basically told fans to chill, he's still chipping away at his next thing. He announced Haunted Chocolatier way back in 2021, and yes, it's been a minute. The man even admitted it feels "kinda dumb" to keep saying the same thing, but here we are. Stardew Valley, the game that somehow still prints money and tops bestseller lists, has been eating up most of his attention, and he's not exactly rushing the follow-up.

And honestly? Reading between the lines of his post, I think that's the point. ConcernedApe isn't some big publisher with ten teams and a roadmap. It's one guy, and he's the kind of person who apparently "tortures" himself over every last detail. He said as much. If you played Stardew Valley, you already know what that obsession produces. That game is bursting at the seams with tiny, thoughtful touches that no rushed studio would bother with. So if he needs another few years to make sure Haunted Chocolatier hits that same standard, who am I to complain?

The downside, of course, is the silence. There's no trailer, no release window, no real concrete anything beyond "it's coming." For a lot of fans, that's rough. We've been waiting since 2021, and the only news is reassurance. But I'd rather wait five more years for something handcrafted than get a hollow sequel cranked out to hit a fiscal quarter. ConcernedApe earned that patience with Stardew, and I'll keep waiting.
