Look, if you've been following Subnautica 2 news, you've probably seen the chaos online when Unknown Worlds said players can't kill predators in the game. People went off, man. But here's the thing — the developers never meant it the way everyone interpreted it.

Design lead Anthony Gallegos just sat down with MinnMax and cleared the air, and honestly, the reasoning makes a lot more sense when you hear it straight from him. The team doesn't want you conquering the ocean. They're not trying to make you some underwater conqueror who slays everything in sight. That's not the vibe Subnautica 2 is going for, at all.

"People have a little bit of a misunderstanding of why we made some of those decisions," Gallegos said. Right, so the whole point was to create something different — a game where you coexist with the environment instead of wiping it out.

Gallegos only joined Unknown Worlds in 2021, so he's relatively new to the team, but he's clearly bought into the vision. Development on Subnautica 2 started just a year after he came on board, which means he's been deep in this philosophy from the beginning.

The game hits early access soon, and this whole predator-killing debate has actually worked out well for them in a weird way. It's got people talking, which means more folks are paying attention to what Unknown Worlds is trying to do differently. Whether you agree with the approach or not, you can't say it isn't making an impact.

Maybe the ocean doesn't need another shooter where you blast everything that moves. Food for thought, lah.
