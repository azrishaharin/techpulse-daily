Okay so remember when smart glasses were just kind of a nerdy curiosity? Yeah, those days are over, and not in a fun way.

According to a new investigation, Meta has reportedly embedded code for an unreleased face-recognition system inside the Meta AI app, and the feature, internally called NameTag, doesn't appear to be enabled yet. Still, the fact that core components were apparently added as early as January is a bit jarring. Meta insists it's still only "exploring the technology," but reading between the lines, this feels pretty far along for something that's just an idea. When your app already contains the scaffolding for a feature, you're not really exploring — you're polishing.

And this lands in an already uncomfortable moment for Ray-Ban Meta glasses. Earlier reporting showed that modders are physically disabling the recording LED on the glasses, which means people can film covertly without that little indicator light giving them away. Cool. Great. Just what everyone wanted: glasses that can quietly record AND, apparently, soon identify the people they're looking at. Even with the LED still working, a face-recognition feature would mean walking down the street and not knowing whether a stranger's eyewear is quietly matching your face to a database.

What's worth watching now is whether Meta actually ships NameTag, and if so, in what form. The company clearly has the technical pieces in place, and the fact that it's been sitting in the app since January suggests this is a "when," not an "if." The bigger question is whether regulators or the public push back hard enough to keep it in the prototype drawer.
