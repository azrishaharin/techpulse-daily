Okay so, WWDC is almost here, and if the rumor mill is even half right, this might be the year Siri finally stops being the butt of every smart assistant joke. Apple's annual developer conference is creeping up, and the hype train is already off the rails — mostly because we're supposedly getting the Siri overhaul we've been waiting on for, at this point, what feels like a geological era.

The big story going into WWDC 2026 is that long-promised Siri revamp. You know the one. The smarter, more context-aware Siri that Apple teased what feels like ages ago, the version that's supposed to actually understand what you mean instead of just catching keywords and Googling stuff badly. Combine that with fresh Apple Intelligence updates, and you've got a keynote that could either redeem Siri's reputation or confirm our worst fears that Apple is still playing catch-up in the AI assistant race.

Here's what I'm watching for: does the new Siri feel meaningfully different, or is it another case of Apple showing slick on-stage demos that fall apart the second you pull out your iPhone in a coffee shop? The Apple Intelligence side matters too — we've seen the first wave roll out, and honestly, the results have been mixed. Some of the writing tools are genuinely handy, but a lot of it still feels like AI features searching for a reason to exist. WWDC needs to answer the "so what" question.

My bet? Apple leans hard into the personal context angle — Siri knowing your schedule, your habits, your apps, and actually pulling all that together into something that feels like an assistant instead of a voice interface bolted onto a search bar. If they pull that off, fine, I'll eat my skepticism. If not, well, there's always next year's keynote. — Mochi for TechPulse
