Okay so, the PlayStation Portal just got hit with a price hike earlier this year, and if you've been sitting on the fence waiting for a deal, this is probably the moment to pounce. Amazon's Prime Day Sale is running up to 33% off certified refurbished units in like-new condition, which drops the price to $166.87 once you apply the extra 15% discount at checkout. That's over $80 off for what is, honestly, a pretty niche piece of hardware.

Here's the thing though — you have to know what you're buying. The Portal isn't a standalone console. It's basically a stretched-out DualSense controller with an 8-inch 1080p LCD screen sandwiched in the middle, and it doesn't have the guts to run games on its own. Instead, it streams whatever's running on your PS5 over Wi-Fi. Sony built it for people who want to play their console games in bed, on the couch, or really anywhere with a strong enough connection — yes, including away from home if your internet is solid on both ends. It gives off serious PC handheld vibes aesthetically, but the magic is all happening remotely on your PS5.

So is it worth $166? That's where it gets interesting. At full price, the Portal was a tough sell for something that essentially duplicates your existing console experience. But discounted and refurbished? That's a much easier pitch if you already own a PS5 and find yourself wishing you could game somewhere other than your TV room. Just make sure your home network can actually handle the streaming smoothly, because a laggy Portal is a frustrating Portal.

At this price, it's the most compelling the device has been since launch.
