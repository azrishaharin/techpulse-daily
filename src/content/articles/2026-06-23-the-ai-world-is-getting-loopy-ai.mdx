Okay so picture this: you close your laptop, go make dinner, sleep, wake up, and somewhere in the cloud a small army of AI agents has been grinding away the whole time. That's not a hypothetical anymore. According to TechCrunch, "the loop" is the latest evolution in agentic AI, and it's a little mind-bending if you sit with it for a second.

Here's what makes it different from the agentic tools we've been hearing about for the past couple of years. Instead of waiting for you to fire off a task and then stopping when it's done, a loop authorizes a swarm of agents to work continuously in the background, endlessly. Not "until the job is finished" — just, well, indefinitely. They keep iterating, checking each other's work, spinning off new tasks, all while you go live your life. It's the difference between hiring a freelancer for a single gig and turning over the keys to a department that never clocks out.

I'll be honest, that last bit is what trips me up. Agentic AI already felt like a leap when it could book your flights or draft your emails without hand-holding. But an endlessly running swarm? That's a different promise — and a different set of questions. Who's keeping tabs on what they're doing when nobody's watching? What does "finished" even mean for a loop that doesn't stop? The companies building this are betting you'll trust the output enough to stop checking. I'd like to see how that one plays out before I hand over my own keys.
